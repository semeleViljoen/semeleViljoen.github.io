Habitat for Humanity (2011)

Trip to Mahalapye, Botswana Building Houses

Travelled with a group of students from my high school all the way to Mahalapye, Botswana through Habitat for Humanity. While we were there we built two houses to near completion. We stayed in the village, met the chief, visited an orphanage, and did some landscaping on their lot.

Competencies gained

  • Mental toughness
  • Physical labor

During this volunteer experience, I learned that

  1. Giving to people that have less than you is extremely rewarding
  2. Building a house is really challenging
  3. I am so lucky to live in Canada and to have everything that I have
  4. Getting along with people you don’t normally associate with is easy and really fun!

Independent (2017-2018)

Individual Senior Aquatherapy

Picking up my client at her house, taking her to the pool, helping her put on her water shoes and getting her into the pool. Once in the pool, taking her through her aquatherapy program, then helping her out of the pool, out of her water shoes and taking her back to her house.

Competencies gained

  • Instructing and conducting aquatherapy
  • Working with someone who isn’t able to walk by herself

During this volunteer experience, I learned that

  1. Taking three hours out of my week can make the world of a difference to one person
  2. Even though we are healthcare professionals, we have to remember that even just talking to someone can make a difference!
  3. I have to remember that I am a young and fit and healthy, and that my clients will not always be at the same ability as I am
  4. If I think something is really easy, this does not mean that it will be easy for everyone
